It was your trusted advisor. Then it started making things up.

You spent hours giving it the full picture, the facts, the decisions, the timeline, trusting it would keep track of all of it. Then it started getting confused. Confidently wrong about things you'd already settled. Dates it couldn't recall. Facts it couldn't keep straight. And once it's lost the thread, there's no getting it back, you're starting over, from square one.

02

This is a problem.

AI is built for conversations, one question, one answer, done. Real work isn't a conversation. It's a project, spanning weeks or months, with facts that change and decisions that build on each other. That's exactly where things start to break down.

And when the details slip, so does what depends on them: a number signed off on that already changed, a deadline missed because the date got lost, hours spent re-solving something you already solved.

03

Why this happens.

This isn't a bad model having a bad day. It's AI amnesia, and it's built into how these systems work. AI doesn't hold onto a conversation the way a person does. Once it runs long, older parts get compressed into a shorter summary to make room for what's new, and every time that happens, something can shift or disappear.

Do that enough times, over weeks or months, and it adds up: dates blur, decisions get restated wrong, facts quietly drift from what was actually true. Your advisor didn't turn unreliable. It got amnesia, and started guessing instead of remembering.
